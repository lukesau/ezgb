# Investigating the 1.05e instability reports

## The question

Was the instability people saw after updating to kernel 1.05e/FW5 a bug in the update itself,
or a consequence of users updating with slow SD cards / weak console batteries?

## What EZ Flash's own release notes establish (paraphrased from their distribution page)

EZ Flash distributed 1.05e/FW5 labeled explicitly as a release candidate, not a final release,
with their own warning attached: updating requires a high-quality, high-speed microSD card,
since a slow/low-quality card during the update itself can brick the cartridge; they also
required fresh or externally-powered batteries in the console during the update. Separately,
and after a successful update, they acknowledged a known runtime bug: consoles with a slower
SD card would hang at the LOADING or OS INIT screen post-upgrade. They recommended staying on
FW4/1.04e unless RTC game support or a specific GBC hardware revision fix was needed.

The changelog's stated goals for this RC were: supporting CGB units without a CPU suffix (tied
to a "Micro SD Initial Error" issue on those units), a rewrite of the RTC handling so it works
correctly with official RTC games, and a faster ("turbo") loading speed on CGB and later
hardware.

This gives the framing: 1.05e was shipped as a release candidate, not a final release, with an
officially acknowledged known issue that matches the "slow SD card" hypothesis, described as a
runtime hang (LOADING/OS INIT, after a successful update), separate from the
bricking-during-the-flash-process warning (about the update procedure itself, not the
resulting kernel's behavior).

Two distinct risk windows, both documented by EZ Flash:
1. During the update: slow card plus weak console batteries can brick the cart (a flashing
   process risk, not a kernel bug).
2. After the update, ongoing: slow card causes a hang at LOADING/OS INIT (an RC-quality
   runtime bug, acknowledged and left unfixed in this build).

Hash-verified: the `ezgb.dat` / `Update_FW5_7-31.gb` in `juniorkernel-1.05e-FW5/` match the
SHA1s EZ Flash posted (`ce1d531...`, `80ef33a...`), confirmed with `shasum` against this repo's
copies. This confirms the analysis covers the genuine RC build, not a corrupted/tampered copy.

## What the disassembly adds

From the 1.04e to 1.05e diff (see `docs/REGISTERS.md` and `docs/DIFF_1.04e_vs_1.05e.md`), the real
logic changes are isolated to bank 0 and bank 1. Several clean insertions in bank 1 (not
present in 1.04e) contain:

- A NOR flash write sequence: `ld bc,$4000 / ld a,$11 / ld [bc],a` (a standard MBC-style
  ROM-bank-select write) immediately followed by `ld bc,$a000 / ld a,$aa / ld [bc],a`. `$AA`
  is the first byte of a JEDEC NOR flash unlock/write command. This lines up with the
  changelog's "RTC codes are rewritten": earlier notes guessed this was NOR flash *instead of*
  a battery RTC. Hardware fact check: the Jr’s **only battery-backed part is the RTC**;
  **FRAM** (saves / `$A300` meta) is non-volatile without a battery. The `$A000=$AA` pattern
  is also the BACKUPSAVE pending stamp in FRAM (`docs/fram-save-map.md`), so treat “JEDEC NOR
  unlock” readings here as unconfirmed / possibly conflated until live-traced.
  (`re/1.05e/disassembly/bank_001.asm` around line 4310-4320, function context starting at
  `Jump_001_5657`.)
- A `TIME:` / `SET` / `AUTO SAVE:` string block in bank 0 (offset `0x11910` in the ROM), a
  SET-menu screen for viewing/setting the clock, confirming there's a user-visible, settable
  time value behind this RTC rewrite.
- The existing `BATTERY` / `DRY!!!` low-battery warning (from 1.04e, offset `0x1911` in the
  ROM) is unrelated to this: it's the console-battery warning already shipped in 1.04e's
  "Added a battery dry notice," not an RTC backup battery indicator.

No CGB-revision hardware registers (`$ff4c`-`$ff77` range) are touched anywhere in either
kernel version. If "CGB without CPU suffix" detection is real, it happens through the cart's
own custom FPGA registers (`$7fxx`, see `docs/REGISTERS.md`), not by reading GBC's own
documented/undocumented CPU registers directly.

## Open questions / not yet confirmed

- How RTC battery backup and any flash/FRAM time image interact (cart coin cell vs console
  AAs during update; which writes in the 1.05e RTC rewrite touch the clock chip vs FRAM).
  FRAM itself does not need the cart battery.
- Whether the "turbo loading speed" change (faster SD clock/timing for CGB+ devices) tightens
  timeout/retry assumptions in a way that would explain hangs specifically on slower cards.
  Haven't yet located the SD wait-loop's timeout constant in 1.05e to compare against 1.04e's
  equivalent. This is the most direct way to confirm or rule out the "runtime hang" mechanism.
- Dynamic tracing in SameBoy is still the fastest way to nail down the actual loader path and
  would likely resolve the open questions above faster than continued static analysis.
